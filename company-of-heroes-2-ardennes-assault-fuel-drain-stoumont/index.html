COMPANY OF HEROES 2—ARDENNES ASSAULT—FUEL DRAIN (STOUMONT)

[2 Nov 2021]

 

This challenging—and potentially catastrophic—mission involves taking and then defending a fuel point against five armor+infantry attack waves, while—if you want a silver or gold medal—a portion of your force roves around taking other well-defended points on the map.

You can use any company for this, though it is preferable that the company have solid artillery to support its roving, point-capping force. I prefer to use Dog Co. for this mission simply because it has good artillery (especially when upgraded with incendiary rounds), and cheaper AT guns and HMGs, and I don’t need anything special in the way of infantry—in fact, I prefer to use a lot of cheap RE squads here because abandoned German schrecks and LMGs, not to mention vehicles, are plentiful.

Assaulting the sanatorium:

Dismiss your riflemen and assault engineers (too expensive in MP).

Bring in an RE squad and the lieutenant squad.

Equip your RE squads with the flamethrower and BAR lying near the base.

With your new squads and your starting RE squad and HMG (and mortar team if you have one), move up the path NNW of your base and set up behind the sandbags. A volksgren squad will come out and attack.

Defeat the volksgren squad that comes out, and the other one inside the big sanatorium building. Heal up, put your HMG in the building and use the rest of your squads to kill remaining units in the VP. Alternatively, delay capping the VP until you have brought in lots of RE squads, wrapped the sanatorium complex with tank traps (TTs), and brought in all your other units.

Defending the sanatorium:

Use two or three AT guns, a couple of HMGs, firing positions with HMGs, squads in the sanatorium building, the Captain squad, and a few RE squads around the flagpole. Add some TTs to hinder enemy armor thrusts if you haven’t already TT-wrapped the complex.

Send some of your RE squads to follow the roving/capping force and grab panzerschrecks they find or de-crew on their journey, bringing them back to the sanatorium. A squad equipped with two schrecks is, with its greater mobility and ability to enter buildings, more effective in this environment than an AT gun.

Note that the southern entrance to the complex has a gate you have to blast away with an AT gun, in order to get AT guns and vehicles into the complex by that route.

There is an abandoned AT gun in the sanatorium point area, so one of your free RE squads can crew that.

Firing positions with HMGs take up a lot of space and hamper the mobility/sight-lines of your other units. Build only one or two within the complex, but others outside, e.g., on the street to the north.

A minute or so after you cap the sanatorium point, a soldier’s voice will cry out “Contact! Enemy advancing along the road!” A Puma will approach, usually from the NNE, followed by two or three infantry units. If blocked by TTs the Puma will come as close as it can and start shooting. This is a scripted event, and although the Puma will do some minor damage, it will almost immediately run out of fuel, and you will be invited to gas it up and take it. Note that while it’s alive the Puma will take no more than 50% damage—I guess the idea the CPU is enforcing here (in Relic’s wonderful immersion-breaking style) is that you’re supposed to capture it, not kill it.

After this, you’ll deal with five attack waves, with delays of a few minutes in between. The earlier attack waves are easier to handle. You don’t need more than two AT guns, but try to have a Captain with bazookas and two or three RE squads with captured schrecks—they are all over the map. You also should have HMGs, infantry units with BARs or LMGs, and a mortar squad or pack howitzer positioned in the parking lot to S of the main point area. If you have taken the other fuel points on the map, the German tanks should run out of fuel more quickly in these attacks.

Note that German tanks can come at the sanatorium from any direction, so try to have all-directional AT capability.

Roving and taking the other two fuel points:

Your initial force for this can be an HMG, Lt. squad (upgraded with dropped LMG from the sanatorium area), mortar team, the captured Puma, two or three flamethrower/LMG/BAR equipped RE squads, and the ambulance.

They should start—asap after the first scripted Puma attack on the sanatorium—by attacking the munitions point nearby to the N. Lt. and flamethrower squad go over the fence and into the small building to the left of the road, HMG on the road, mortar well back so it can hit everything. You will face four or five mostly volksgren squads.

When that muni point is capped and your force is healed up, attack the fuel point just down the hill to the E. There will be volksgrens, including some with schrecks, and panzerfusiliers with LMGs.

Repeat for the territory point about 100 m east. Caution: While you are in this point, German armor and infantry may pass on the adjacent road—back off if they do.

Cross the road and approach the other fuel point very cautiously. There will be elite units plus a Pak and HMG. Sometimes there is a crippled Panzer IV to the north of the north entrance of the fuel point—you have to deal with that first. Use mortar and artillery on the Pak and HMG in the fuel point when you have some visibility. Keep your HMG in the building near the just-mentioned entrance. More German units, including a fallschirmjaeger squad and a Pak, will attack from the E when you enter the fuel point area, so be ready with HMG, flamethrower, mortar etc. This will be a tough fight.

Bring some of your units back to the sanatorium, cautiously. The Puma and a few other strong units can, with the help of artillery, continue around the map attempting side-missions or just building up XP by wiping German infantry.

Side-mission:

The kill-crippled-tank side-mission you have to undertake before assaulting the second fuel point. The kill-VIP side-mission is best done afterwards—poke with Puma to reveal the VIP, back off and let your call-in artillery rain down.

Other notes:

  • It’s tempting to man lots of abandoned German armor, but they are hard to handle in the crowded sanatorium space, and the German attack waves have enormous anti-AT power so you are, in a sense, only giving them a better target. Well-equipped infantry in cover are generally better for your defense against the late, strong, German attack waves.
  • Many German vehicles are immune to your AT fire or won’t go below a certain damage level—I guess the best thing to do is avoid them till they run out of gas.
  • There is no German base you can kill.
  • There are sometimes bugs affecting mortars (won’t fire or auto-fire), HMGs in buildings (won’t auto-face), and tank refueling (you click but refueling doesn’t happen). If you line the map-edge with TTs, you may block some German vehicles, especially in the woods to the E of the sanatorium point, and until you send a unit to be seen by them, they will be “stuck” and the mission won’t progress to the next attack wave. When the mission fails to progress, check the map for these ghost units.
