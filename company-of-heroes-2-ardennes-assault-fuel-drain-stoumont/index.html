COMPANY OF HEROES 2—ARDENNES ASSAULT—FUEL DRAIN (STOUMONT)

[2 Nov 2021]

 

This challenging—and potentially catastrophic—mission involves taking and then defending a fuel point against five armor+infantry attack waves, while—if you want a silver or gold medal—a portion of your force roves around taking other well-defended points on the map.

You can use any company for this, though it is preferable that the company have solid artillery to support its roving, point-capping force. I prefer to use Dog Co. for this mission simply because it has good artillery even when un-upgraded, and cheaper AT guns and HMGs, and I don’t need anything special in the way of infantry—in fact, I prefer to use a lot of cheap RE squads here because abandoned German schrecks and LMGs, not to mention vehicles, are plentiful.

Assaulting the sanatorium:

Use your starting HMG, riflemen, an RE squad with a BAR (lying on the ground near your base), and a separate RE squad with flamethrower (ditto). They go from your base to the back gate/entrance of the sanatorium, where they set up and fire, killing one volksgren squad on foot and then another (get close with flamethrower) in the main building.

When you’re done, put your HMG in the building. It will wipe the German squad standing out in the open in the main sanatorium fuel point area and suppress any others that are in range.

Do not cap the sanatorium point.

Setting up your defense of the sanatorium

Dismiss your riflemen and assault engineers (too expensive in MP).

Bring in about 5 RE squads and use them with your other RE squads to start wrapping the sanatorium complex with tank traps (TTs). Leave no gaps (you will learn the hard way how to do this).

Bring in an ambulance and put it at the bottom of the road heading to the nearby munitions point. Lay TT so the ambulance is outside the TT.

Bring in a Lt. and Captain, two more HMGs, an AT gun, two mortar teams.

Use the Lt., with HMG support from the sanatorium building, to wipe the remaining German squads in the sanatorium fuel point area (otherwise they will kill your RE squads).

Only when you are done laying TT should you cap the sanatorium point.

When you cap the point, grab the fuel/muni caches and weapons lying around.

Build FPs (upgrade asap) in the point area and on the nearby road, to cover enemy approaches.

The Puma attacks

A minute or so after you cap the sanatorium point, a soldier’s voice will cry out “Contact! Enemy advancing along the road!” A Puma will approach, usually from the NNE, followed by two or three infantry units. If blocked by TTs the Puma will come as close as it can and start shooting. This is a scripted event, and although the Puma may do some minor damage, it will almost immediately run out of fuel, and you will be invited to gas it up and take it. Note that while it’s alive the Puma will take no more than 50% damage—I guess the idea the CPU is enforcing here (in Relic’s wonderful immersion-breaking style) is that you’re supposed to capture it, not kill it. So do that.

Your roving force

Your initial force for this can be an HMG, Lt. squad (upgraded with dropped LMG from the sanatorium area), mortar teams, the captured Puma, at least a couple of RE squads with flamethrowers and LMGs, and the ambulance.

They should start—asap after the initial Puma attack—by attacking the munitions point nearby to the N. Lt. and flamethrower squad go over the fence and into the small building to the left of the road, HMG on the road, mortars well back so they can hit everything. You will face four or five mostly volksgren squads.

When that muni point is capped and your force is healed up, attack the fuel point just down the hill to the E. There will be volksgrens, including some with schrecks, and panzerfusiliers with LMGs. Early placement of your HMG on the slope is key.

Repeat for the territory point about 100 m east. Caution: While you are in this point, German armor and infantry may pass on the adjacent road—back off if they do.

Cross the road and approach the other fuel point very cautiously. There will be elite units plus a Pak and HMG. Sometimes there is a side-mission crippled Panzer IV to the north of the north entrance of the fuel point—you have to deal with that first.

Keep your HMG in the building to the left of the north entrance to the fuel point.

To see into the fuel point to spot for your mortar teams, have your Puma nose up to the left side of it.

When you enter that fuel point, more German units, including a fallschirmjaeger squad and a Pak, will attack from the E, so be ready with HMG, flamethrower, mortar etc. This will be a tough fight.

When you’ve capped that second fuel point and healed up your squads, move W to the remaining muni point and take that, again using your Puma to spot for your mortars/artillery.

Now dismiss your ambulance, since it can’t get back through the TTs. Bring your infantry units back to the sanatorium to join the defense.

The Puma can, with the help of artillery, continue around the map attempting side-missions or just building up XP by wiping German infantry.

Meanwhile, defend the sanatorium:

Initially use one or two AT guns, a couple of HMGs, FPs with HMGs, the Captain squad, an RE squad in the sanatorium building for better visibility, and a few RE squads with schrecks and other scrounged weapons (MG42 LMGs and HMGs are good) they can collect around the map as the mission unfolds (some of your RE squads can follow your fuel-point-capping team).

Always use the down-time between attack waves to add TTs on roads/paths to keep enemy armor from getting into range to harm your sanatorium defense force. It’s especially important to block armor attacks from the W side of the complex—and that part of the map opens up to permit TT-laying only after the Puma attack.

Note that the southern entrance to the complex has a gate you have to blast away with an AT gun, in order to get AT guns and vehicles into the complex by that route.

There is an abandoned AT gun in the sanatorium point area, so one of your free RE squads can crew that.

Note that German tanks can come at the sanatorium from any direction, so try to have all-directional AT capability.

Side-mission:

The kill-crippled-tank side-mission you have to undertake before assaulting the second fuel point. The kill-VIP side-mission is at the top of the map and is best done with your Puma after capping the second fuel point—poke to reveal the VIP, back off and let your call-in artillery rain down. The gather-intel side mission is easy in this mission, since you should get full map control by mid-game.

Other notes:

  • It’s tempting to man lots of abandoned German armor, but they are hard to handle in the crowded sanatorium space, and the German attack waves have enormous anti-AT power so you are, in a sense, only giving them a better target. Well-equipped infantry in cover are generally better for your defense.
  • Most German vehicles are immune to your AT fire or won’t go below a certain damage level, but if you have capped all fuel points, they usually run out of gas within 10-20 seconds of being spotted.
  • There is no German base you can kill.
  • There are sometimes bugs affecting mortars (won’t fire or auto-fire), HMGs in buildings (won’t auto-face), and tank refueling (you click but refueling doesn’t happen). If you lay down a lot of TTs, you may block some German vehicles, especially in the woods to the W and E of the sanatorium point, and until you send a unit to be seen by them, they will be “stuck” and the mission won’t progress to the next attack wave. When the mission fails to progress, check the map for these ghost units.
  • You should finish this mission with 70+ XP and few or no casualties.
