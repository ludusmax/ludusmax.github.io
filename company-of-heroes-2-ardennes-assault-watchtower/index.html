COMPANY OF HEROES 2—ARDENNES ASSAULT—WATCHTOWER

[2 Nov 2021]

 

This can be one of the most difficult/unbalanced missions in Ardennes Assault, and your chances of getting gold—even on level 1 difficulty—often boil down to dumb luck. So be ready to exit to Windows or quit the program in Task Manager at any time to start over.

The concept in “Watchtower” is that you have to capture German aerial supply drops, which come every few minutes, starting very early in the mission, near one of three watchtowers in German territory. If you hold a given watchtower, you can see the surrounding area better, and can spot the supply drops before “Jerry.” Each side’s victory points tick down relentlessly unless it captures one of the supply drops, in which case its points go up a bit. So by capturing more supply drops than the Germans, you can force them to go to zero before you do. To win gold you must capture the first six supply drops—if you do that, the Germans will hit zero points before the 7th drop comes. If the Germans capture any drop at all . . . no gold for you. Thus, the key to winning gold is capturing and holding all three watchtower points and keeping some of your units close to them—preferably in buildings, or otherwise in cover.

This is not always easy or even doable, since you have to be ready to cap and contest all three watchtower points immediately, and any American unit that you can get out in significant numbers early-game is going to be, of course, pathetically weak. Even unupgraded Rangers will take heavy casualties or be snuffed completely when close to (SMG-equipped) Sturmpios. Also, the map is crawling from the start with kubelwagens, against which unupgraded American companies have no early-game defense. Worse, it sometimes happens that on some maps and higher difficulty levels, the very first drop, just a minute or two into the mission, lands just outside the German base, near a watchtower that you have zero chance of holding at that early stage. (When that happens, just start the mission over.)

Another big problem hampering your ability to do well in this mission is the fact that you have to focus on capping and holding all three watchtowers, which leaves you initially with no ability to hold other points to get munitions and fuel—which in turn means your units can’t get the weapons they need to survive.

Added to all that is the fact that this is a quick mission—if you get the first six drops it always takes exactly the same amount of time for the Germans to run to zero points—so even though you may take heavy casualties, you won’t be able to build significant XP/veterancy.

All that said, if you can get that first drop, then—certainly on difficulty levels 1 and 2—you should be able to build up your strength to get the next five, most of the time. The German units are sometimes (unrealistically) slow to grab the drops, even when they are right next to them. On level three and up, however, you should not attempt this mission without a veteran/upgraded company.
